# sales_data-analysis
A data analysis project using Excel for data from BlinkIT, a food products company. Initially, the data was entered into a Power Query because it needed cleaning, and there was also missing weight data, which was replaced with averages.
Incorrect data was replaced with correct data.

I ensured that all data was accurate and clear. Then, I used pivot tables to generate the necessary reports for analysis and to answer the analytical questions. The final step is a dashboard that presents the information in a simple and understandable way.

Blinkit Grocery Dashboard

1. Total Sales by Year
Sales saw gradual growth from 2011 to 2013.

Between 2014 and 2017, sales remained relatively stable.

A significant surge occurred in 2018, marking the highest sales figure.

From 2019 to 2022, a decline in performance or a slowdown in growth was observed.

🔎 Conclusion: The peak was in 2018. There is a possibility of strong competition or a change in consumer behavior afterward.

2. Items by Content
65% of the products are low-fat.

35% of products are regular.

🔎 Conclusion: The company's overall direction aligns with customer preferences for healthy options, which is a significant competitive advantage.

3. Items by Outlet Size:
43% of products are in medium-sized stores.

37% are in large stores.

Only 20% are in small stores.

🔎 Conclusion: There is a greater focus on medium and large stores, and consideration should be given to supporting small stores with more products.

4. Total Sales and Rating by Item Type:
The highest sales were in categories such as:

Dairy

Frozen Foods

Canned Foods

Categories such as Soft Drinks and Breakfast have high ratings but low sales.

🔎 Conclusion: Certain categories could improve sales through better marketing because they have high customer ratings.

5. Weight and Visibility by Item Type
Categories such as Dairy, Fruits, and Vegetables have the highest weight and visibility.

Categories such as Breads and Breakfast have low weight and visibility.

Conclusion: Some low-visibility categories need redistribution or in-store marketing.

6. Number of Outlets by Location and Type
The most prevalent type is Supermarket Type 1 with 2,785 stores.

Followed by Supermarket Type 2 with 1,860 stores.

Small stores have a lower prevalence (Grocery Stores - only 528).

Distribution between Tier 1, Tier 2, and Tier 3:

The greatest concentration is in Tier 2.

Tier 3 has moderate representation.

Tier 1 has the least representation.

Conclusion: Coverage of rural and urban markets in Tier 2 is good. However, there is an opportunity to improve coverage in major cities (Tier 1).

✅ Suggested Recommendations for Performance Improvement
1- Analyze the reasons for the slowdown after 2018, such as increased competition or changes in demand.

2- Support small shops with essential products, especially dairy, frozen foods, and fast-moving consumer goods.

3- Increase visibility for high-rated products, such as beverages and breakfast items, by displaying them prominently or in advertising campaigns.

4- Expand into major cities (Tier 1) by opening new branches or offering express delivery to compensate for limited physical presence.

5- Promote high-quality, low-selling products through seasonal offers and discounts.
